# The Price of Power — data notes

The chart transcribes the “Selected Historical Average LCOE Values” panel in Lazard's **Levelized Cost of Energy+**, version 18.0, published June 2025.

Source: <https://www.lazard.com/research-insights/levelized-cost-of-energyplus/>

The comparison covers 2009–2025 and omits 2022, when Lazard did not publish a new LCOE edition. Each value is the average of the high and low unsubsidized levelized cost estimate for that technology in that edition, expressed as dollars per megawatt-hour. Values are shown as reported and are not converted into constant dollars.

The seven transcribed technologies are utility-scale solar PV, onshore wind, combined-cycle gas, coal, U.S. nuclear, geothermal, and gas peaking. The chart does not use tax-credit cases.

LCOE spreads lifetime plant-level cost over expected electricity generation. It does not measure the full electricity-system value or cost of a technology. In particular, it excludes transmission, distribution, reliability value, and much of the cost of balancing variable output. Technologies also provide different services and are not interchangeable simply because they share an axis.

Lazard notes that recent new-build coal and nuclear observations are limited. Its displayed nuclear series after version 14.0 reflects that edition's results adjusted for inflation and is based on then-estimated Vogtle costs.
