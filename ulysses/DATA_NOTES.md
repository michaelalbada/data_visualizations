# Ulysses data notes

The visualization uses the public-domain Project Gutenberg edition of James Joyce's *Ulysses*, eBook 4300, downloaded from `https://www.gutenberg.org/cache/epub/4300/pg4300.txt` on July 22, 2026. Gutenberg notes that its text is based on pre-1923 print editions.

The eighteen episode boundaries are the numbered section markers in that text. Word counts use letter-based tokens, including internal apostrophes and hyphens. Distinct-word counts are case-insensitive. Paragraph counts use blank-line-separated text blocks; punctuation counts are literal question and exclamation marks. These measurements are reproducible for this file but can differ from other editions and tokenizers.

Episode titles, scenes, hours, organs, arts, symbols, and techniques follow the Gilbert schema that Joyce produced in November 1921. The schema does not assign an hour to “Penelope”; the spiral places it schematically just after “Ithaca” for legibility and labels it “after 2 a.m.”

The short episode synopses, Homeric mirrors, character lists, motifs, and formal notes are editorial summaries cross-checked against Patrick Hastings's UlyssesGuide episode guides and the public-domain novel text. They are interpretive reading aids, not additional quantitative data.
